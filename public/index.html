About Me

NOTE: This is printed version of www.oangelov.com on

"The best way to predict the future is to invent it." —Alan Kay

I have been haunted by this quote ever since I first read it. Every time when I get bored it pops in my head and it doesn't leave until I have done something meaningful.

I believe every person has different strengths and weaknesses. Those traits should be explored in order to find the right path for that individual. That path is the one that offers opportunities to challenge their strengths and chances to work on their weaknesses, as well as the one that allows for the most natural, fun and effective way to live.
I think I am on the right track in finding my path. Ever since I was a child I have loved exploring how different things work (and yes - this meant a lot of broken stuff), as well as creating something useful by combining and modifying existing objects. And last but not least - I have loved "hacking" tedious tasks by coming up with original ways to do it (e.g. when I was doing letter writing exercises I did two lines at the time by using two pencils at once - therefore my handwriting suffered).
Those passions for exploring and inventing made me better at exact sciences like math and physics. Also, I was absolutely absorbed by gadgets, pocket and video games and eventually everything to do with computers. So by the time I had to make a decision about what to study after school it was pretty obvious that: math + computers = programming.
After I made this key career choice I have been, naturally, following it. While doing so I'm trying different activities to improve as a person (that is why I have so many hobbies) - snowboarding, tennis, swimming, photography, music (drums and flute) and many more.
Lately I'm very interested in machine learning and data science as a whole. In the past 6 months I've dedicated myself to reading articles, following courses and exploring datasets.

FMI is essential place for the software industry in Sofia. Here most companies seek passionate students for work. There is a traditional hackathon called HackFMI I always participate in order to get motivation, challenges and learn something new (I met Chobolab's CTO on the first HackFMI when he was mentoring).
In FMI I found about Obecto. I joined the company, while continuing studing.
